Uniquely Penn

Liberal Arts in Practice

From 50 states and 137 countries, Penns 10,000 undergraduates pursue studies in the natural sciences, social sciences, and humanities alongside pre-professional preparation through Penn Engineering, the School of Nursing, and the Wharton School of Business.

One Unified Campus

Penns physical and intellectual connections encourage shared learning experiences. A walkable campus links the resources of 4 undergraduate and 12 graduate schools, 3 teaching hospitals, and 142 research centers.

91 undergraduate majors

At Home In Philadelphia

Penn is fully integrated with the nations fifth-largest city. Beautiful and bustling, the 302-acre campus is a fifteen-minute walk from Center CityPhiladelphias hub of culture, commerce, and cuisine.

80% of Penn nursing students pursue one of Penn Nursing Science's 20 master's degree programsand have 75 percent of their graduate tuition covered by the University of Pennsylvania Health System and Penn.

Academics

Premiere Education in Nursing

Penn Nursing students combine a liberal arts and sciences foundation with intensive clinical experiences, enjoying partnerships with more than 300 health care facilities, including the nations top-ranked childrens hospital.

Practice As You Learn

Nursing students complete at least 864 hours of clinical rotations, learning in partnership with the nations top nurse educators. Their learning and experiences are enhanced through research, overseas study, and involvement with public health outreach initiatives.

Nursing Outcomes

80% of Penn Nursing graduates pursue one of Penn Nursing Sciences 20 masters degrees programs. In demand and commanding competitive salaries, Penn alumni are health care practice, policy, and research leaders.

8:1 nursing student-to-faculty ratio in clinical settings

Penn's global alumni network of 297,000 (and growing) means instant contacts and connections that span every industry and walk of life.

Financial Aid

A Best Value

Penn is among Kiplingers top 10 Best Values in Private Universities, a ranking that factors tuition, quality of education, and generosity of aid.

$44,772 average aid award for aided incoming freshmen in 2013

Graduate debt-free

Penn makes it possible for its students to graduate without debt by:

  • excluding loans from aid packages
  • devoting $180 million annually to undergraduate aid
  • offering generous aid policies (83% of incoming freshmen who applied for aid received assistance)

We Meet Full Demonstrated Need

Penn opens its doors to the worlds best minds by:

  • offering a need-blind admission policy (admitting the most qualified applicants regardless of their familys ability to pay)
  • meeting the full need of all admitted U.S. citizens and permanent residents (and those of Canada and Mexico)
